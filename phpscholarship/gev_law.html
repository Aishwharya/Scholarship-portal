Eligibility Criteria

The applicant must follow the following eligibility criteria to apply for the GEV memorial merit scholarship 2022

An applicant must be an Indian citizen

The applicant must be already enrolled in any year of an LLB/LLM degree course at a recognized law institute in India, or have applied for CLAT, LSAT-India, AILET, or any other law entrance exam.

An applicant must have scored a minimum of 60% in Class X and XII board exams.

The applicant must have an annual family income of not more than INR 10,00,000 from all sources.

The applicant must be willing to sign-up for the scholarship fund’s annual mentorship programs to assist and nurture the next batches of GEV Merit Scholars.

Documents Required

The following documents must be submit to apply for this scholarship program

Copies of the mark sheets (each qualifying examination from Secondary School Certificate examination to the latest qualification)

Copy of the letter of admission to the Law College, Institution, or University at which the applicant is enroll or intends to undertake studies (optional)

Income proof (Salary Statement, IT Returns) of applicant’s parents

Copy of latest CLAT/LSAT-India/AILET/other law entrance exam results

Passport size photograph

Scanned copy of Aadhar Card

Selection Procedure

The students selection is based on a multi-stage procedure presented by the concerned authorities of the scholarship program and also the main motive to select the students is to provide them with financial assistance. The students who are doing well in their studies will select on the basis of their financial needs. First of all initial shortlisting of the students based on their academic merit and financial need will be done by the concern authorities. The students will have to also go through a telephonic round of interviews and then all of the documents will be verified by the student. The student will also have to go through a personal interview after which a final selection will be done by the concern authorities.
