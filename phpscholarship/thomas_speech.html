Sir Thomas Naghten Fitzgerald Scholarship

Scholarship Link

This scholarship, worth up to $7,200, is for domestic medical graduates undertaking a graduate research degree in surgery through the Faculty of Medicine, Dentistry and Health Sciences. It was established with a bequest from the late Kathleen Maggie Douglas in memory of her father.

Applicable study areas

Medicine, dentistry, health and welfare

It is provided as follows

1. The sum of $50,080 and any accumulations and additions to the sum forms a fund called the "Sir Thomas Naghten Fitzgerald Scholarship" (“the fund”) and the fund must be paid into an investment pool and remain there until the Council directs otherwise.

2. (1) The value of the scholarship is that which the Council may determine from time to time.

(2) Income from the fund not required for the award of a scholarship in any year may by the direction of the Council be appropriated to an income account to be available for a later award of a scholarship.

3. The purpose of the scholarship is to enable medical graduates to further their surgical training by study in Australia or overseas.

4. The award may be made annually by the Council on the advice of the medical research committee of the faculty of Medicine, Dentistry and Health Sciences, having in mind the advancement of Surgery in this school.

5. The scholarship may be held concurrently with any other award.
